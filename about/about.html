About This Research Portal

A computational framework for transforming archival materials into structured data, spatial systems, and interactive research infrastructure.

Overview

This research portal develops computational methods for working with archival materials, with a focus on scanned documents, historical brochures, and spatial records. It combines optical character recognition (OCR), natural language processing (NLP), and geospatial analysis to convert unstructured historical sources into structured datasets and interactive systems.

This project treats archives not as static collections, but as computational inputs for mapping, modeling, and interpreting historical space, labor, and cultural infrastructure.

Research Areas

The work is organized across three interconnected domains:

Document Intelligence Systems
OCR pipelines, layout parsing, and structured extraction from scanned archival materials.

Spatial & GIS Systems
Geocoding historical records, reconstructing business networks, and building spatial narratives from archival datasets.

Data Systems & Dashboards
Interactive interfaces for exploring migration data, archival corpora, and computational research outputs.

Methods

The system integrates Python-based OCR pipelines (PyMuPDF, Tesseract, OpenCV), data structuring workflows (pandas, regex-based parsing), and geospatial tools (Folium, GeoJSON, ArcGIS). These components are designed to move between image, text, and spatial representation.

Rather than treating OCR as a preprocessing step, this work treats it as a form of interpretation—where layout, typography, and spatial structure carry meaning that can be computationally modeled.

Data Philosophy

The project is grounded in the idea that archival materials encode spatial and social relations. By structuring these materials computationally, it becomes possible to trace networks of labor, migration, tourism, and institutional development across time.

The goal is not only digitization, but reconfiguration: turning archives into systems that can be queried, mapped, and reassembled into new forms of historical understanding.

Technical Stack

Python (OCR + data pipelines), OpenCV, PyMuPDF, Tesseract, pandas, regex processing, GeoPandas, Folium, ArcGIS StoryMaps, and lightweight JavaScript for interface design.
