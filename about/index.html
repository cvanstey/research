Cynthia Anstey

Data Scientist · Oral Historian · Computational Archivist

Combining spatial data, census records, and machine learning with archival methods and oral history practice to surface the communities most often left out of the picture.

I graduated with a degree in Literature from Stockton University in 2022, then returned to earn a Master of Arts in American Studies in December 2024. I'm currently completing my Master of Science in Data Science and Analytics at Stockton. Fluent in both English and Italian, I approach data the way I approach texts — with close reading, historical context, and a commitment to the communities whose lives the numbers represent.

My background in technical support gave me early hands-on experience with data: extracting, transforming, and loading across systems long before I had formal statistical training. Today I work primarily in R and Python, alongside SQL and geospatial tooling. As an oral history practitioner, projects have spanned photo restoration, transcription, translation, digital archiving, grant writing, and spatial and predictive modeling.
