About This Research Portal

A computational research system for transforming archival materials into structured datasets, spatial models, and interactive analytical infrastructure.

Overview

This project develops computational methods for working with archival materials, especially scanned brochures, historical documents, and spatial records. It combines optical character recognition (OCR), natural language processing (NLP), and geospatial analysis to convert unstructured archives into structured datasets.

Archives are treated here not as static collections, but as computational systems that encode spatial, economic, and cultural relationships.

Research Areas

Document Intelligence Systems OCR pipelines, layout parsing, and structured extraction from scanned archival materials.
Spatial & GIS Systems Geocoding historical records and reconstructing spatial networks of labor, tourism, and migration.
Data Systems & Dashboards Interactive interfaces for exploring archival datasets and computational research outputs.

Methods

The system integrates Python-based OCR pipelines (PyMuPDF, OpenCV, Tesseract), data structuring workflows (pandas, regex parsing), and geospatial tools (GeoPandas, Folium, ArcGIS).

OCR is treated not only as extraction, but as interpretation: layout, spacing, and typography are analyzed as structured signals that encode meaning.

Data Philosophy

Archival materials are treated as encoded spatial systems. By structuring them computationally, it becomes possible to reconstruct networks of movement, labor, and institutional development across time.

The goal is not digitization alone, but reconfiguration: turning archives into systems that can be queried, mapped, and recomposed.

Technical Stack

Python, PyMuPDF, Tesseract, OpenCV, pandas, regex processing, GeoPandas, Folium, ArcGIS StoryMaps, and lightweight JavaScript interfaces.
