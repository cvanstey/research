Predictive Pipeline System

Descriptive and Inferential predictive modeling pipeline to assess food landscapes in New Jersey.

MLGIS

What It Models

ZIP-code-level food access across New Jersey, combining demographic, economic, and retail-access variables (median income, % elderly, % poverty, % no-vehicle households, distance to nearest supermarket, SNAP/WIC retailer density) into composite vulnerability scores and risk tiers.

Two Linked Analyses

  • Elderly food access vulnerability — a composite score combining distance-to-supermarket, vehicle access, poverty, and elderly population share, sorted into four risk tiers (Low → Critical)
  • SNAP/WIC retailer gap analysis — ZIPs where poverty/SNAP-eligibility is high but SNAP and WIC outlet density per 10k residents is near zero, flagged as "combined gap zips"

Notable Findings

Elderly-heavy ZIPs in the Critical Risk tier average 9.94% no-vehicle households and 3.97 miles to the nearest supermarket — nearly double the no-vehicle rate and more than double the distance of Low Risk ZIPs. Burlington and Middlesex counties rank highest for elderly food vulnerability; several SNAP/WIC "combined gap" ZIPs (e.g. Readington Twp, High Bridge Boro) have zero SNAP or WIC outlets despite measurable poverty rates.

Spearman correlation against county health data showed % elderly population is moderately correlated with high cholesterol (r=0.66) and coronary heart disease (r=0.40) prevalence, but not significantly correlated with diabetes or depression rates — suggesting food-access vulnerability tracks more closely with certain chronic conditions than others.
